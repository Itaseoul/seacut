# Trademark & Badge Policy

The open licenses (CERN-OHL-S for hardware, MIT for firmware, CC BY-SA for docs, CC BY for data)
free the **designs, code, docs, and data**. The **names and badges** — "SEA:CUT", "Friendly Floaty",
and the "Friendly Floaty Compatible" badge — are governed separately, on the Precious Plastic model:
**the marks are conditional on safety and recovery, and may be revoked on violation.** A permissive
data license cannot police safety; this policy gives the safety rules teeth.

## You MAY, without asking
- Build, modify, manufacture, sell, and deploy units under the open licenses.
- Describe your build as "based on" / "derived from" SEA:CUT / Friendly Floaty (accurate attribution).
- Fork the docs and translate them (CC BY-SA / CC BY terms).

## You MAY use the "Friendly Floaty Compatible" badge only if
- Your variant self-declares **all four kernels** — K1 poolable schema · K2 recover-first ·
  K3 verified float · K4 honest labels — **and attaches per-unit hash evidence** (DIY_DIVERSITY §4.2).
- You follow **recover-first** (count-in = count-out; access-class C = no release) and **cell safety**
  (protected Li-ion, low-temp charge cutoff, no toxics, cell-failure containment).
- You keep the **honest labels**: cellular ≠ satellite · physics/positioning ≠ learned AI · all
  buoyancy/power/drift figures are estimates · effort (litter picked) ≠ pollution total.

## The marks are revoked if
- A build is presented as "Friendly Floaty" / "SEA:CUT" while breaking recover-first, cell safety, or
  the honest labels; or emits data under the name **without passing the provenance gate** (§4.1);
- The "Compatible" badge is claimed without per-unit evidence (that is the `unverified` state, not
  Compatible).

Enforcement is social and mechanical, not surveillance: a non-compliant build is simply "not official
Friendly Floaty and not in the shared dataset." Mark disputes: 사단법인 이타서울 (Itaseoul) / GAA.
