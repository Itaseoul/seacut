Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International license (CC BY-SA 4.0).

You may share and adapt for any purpose, including commercially, provided you (1) give
appropriate credit and (2) license your adaptations under the same CC BY-SA terms.

under closed terms. This mirrors the hardware's reciprocal (CERN-OHL-S) stance.

Full text: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-sa/4.0/

## Data — CC BY 4.0 (Attribution)

The observation **data** — the schema and sample data in `data/`, and the trajectories
POSTed to the shared map — is licensed under CC BY 4.0 (Attribution).

You may share, adapt, **and aggregate** for any purpose, including commercially, provided
you give appropriate credit. Data is intentionally **permissive (BY, not ShareAlike)** so
pooled citizen-science observations can be freely combined with other datasets and cited
without relicensing friction — the reuse a source-to-sea data commons depends on, and the
convention of peer projects (PlasticPirates / Zenodo, GBIF, iNaturalist). This is the K1
"poolable data" kernel made legal: the value is in the shared, citable standard, not in
enclosing the records.

Full text: https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by/4.0/
