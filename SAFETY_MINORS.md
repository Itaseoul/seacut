# Safety — Minors & School Deployments

**River water + lithium cells + minors** is the highest-risk combination this project touches. These
rules are **mandatory** for any deployment involving under-18 participants and sit on top of the field
SOP ([hardware/PRELAUNCH_RECOVERY_CHECKLIST.md](hardware/PRELAUNCH_RECOVERY_CHECKLIST.md)) and the cell
safety rules (kernel K3 / [hardware/RELIABILITY.md](hardware/RELIABILITY.md)).

## Non-negotiable
- **No water entry by students.** Students release, observe, and record from inside a railing / deck
  only. **Recovery is done by adults.** The default is an **indoor tank / school pond** release; open
  water is an option *only* when the PRELAUNCH access-class gate passes (A/B; C = no release).
- **Lithium & soldering are adult-only.** Charging, sealing, and any soldering are handled by staff.
  Protected cell only; no charging below 0 °C or heating above 45 °C; contain a single-cell failure.
- **Supervision ratio** ≥ ~1 adult : 6 students near any waterside; a qualified safety officer
  (CPR / water-rescue) per class wherever open water is used; life jackets for the near-water group.
- **Consent & imaging.** Guardian consent for participation and for any images; **no student PII** in
  the data commons ([PRIVACY.md](PRIVACY.md)).
- **Insurance.** Event liability + participant accident insurance; the whole release/recovery is run
  under teacher supervision and documented.
- **Stop triggers.** Rain/flow/ice forecasts abort the waterside activity → switch to the remote /
  tank form. Recover 100% (count-in = count-out).

## School-record (세특) note — Korea
A student-record (세특) entry is possible **only when a teacher runs the program as regular class**
(subject / 창체 / 자유학기). External-instructor delivery and external awards are **not** record-eligible.
Deliver as **teacher-run** (with an optional short online pre-lesson), never as an outside contest.

The lowest-risk form is the **remote / non-waterside** variant: staff pre-release real FF-ID units and
students study the live trajectory in the classroom — no water access, no minors near lithium in water.
